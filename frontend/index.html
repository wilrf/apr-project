NFL Upset Prediction

Can three different AI approaches reveal what makes NFL upsets predictable?

Research Question: How do the agreement and disagreement patterns of three structurally distinct ML architectures reveal the mechanisms and predictability boundaries of NFL upsets?
LR Logistic Regression

The simplest model. Looks at a snapshot summary of each team (win rate, scoring averages, Elo rating, etc.) and makes a single yes/no decision. Like reading a stat sheet before the game.

XGB XGBoost

A more complex model. Gets the same stats as LR plus each team's last 3 games in detail. It finds combinations of factors that matter together—like "a team that's 2–8 AND playing on a short week."

LSTM Long Short-Term Memory Neural Network

A neural network that watches the sequence of each team's last 8 games. Designed to pick up on streaks, momentum shifts, and trajectory. Like watching game film instead of reading box scores.

Key terms used in this dashboard

Upset
= When the expected loser (underdog by 3+ points on the betting line) wins the game.
AUC
= How well a model ranks games by upset risk. 0.5 = coin flip, 1.0 = perfect. Higher is better.
Brier Score
= How accurate the predicted probabilities are. Lower is better (0 = perfect).
Threshold
= A model "predicts upset" when its probability exceeds the overall upset rate (~30%), not 50%.
